Phoenix, Arizona has emerged as one of the most dynamic business cities in the American Southwest. Driven by a decade of population growth, corporate relocations from California, and a diversifying economy spanning finance, technology, healthcare, and real estate, Phoenix now boasts a coworking and private office market that rivals many coastal metros. The city's combination of affordable real estate, a growing professional class, and year-round sunshine has made it a magnet for entrepreneurs, remote workers, and established firms alike.

This guide ranks the 13 best coworking spaces in Phoenix, AZ for 2026, covering options from budget-friendly hot desks to premium private executive suites — with pricing ranging from approximately $20/day for drop-in access to $700+/month for full-service private offices. Whether you're a solo consultant, an attorney building a practice, or a distributed team looking for a professional home base in the Valley of the Sun, Phoenix has a workspace that fits.

2 Industrious — Phoenix

Industrious is widely regarded as the gold standard for premium coworking in the U.S., and its Phoenix presence delivers that standard to the Valley market. The brand's signature all-inclusive model — utilities, internet, coffee, cleaning, printing, and meeting room credits all bundled into the monthly rate — eliminates the add-on cost frustration common at other providers.

Private suites are beautifully appointed with high-end finishes and hospitality-grade service is a genuine differentiator. Industrious starts around $500–$800/month for private offices. For teams that need a premium brand environment and are willing to pay for it, Industrious consistently delivers an excellent experience.

3 WeWork — Phoenix

WeWork maintains a notable presence in the Phoenix market, bringing its globally recognized brand and all-access membership model to the Valley. The spaces follow WeWork's signature open-plan, energetic aesthetic — ideal for networking, startup culture, and creative work, though less suited for confidential or client-facing professional environments.

WeWork's all-access membership tiers are a genuine value for professionals who travel frequently and want a consistent workspace across U.S. cities. Private offices are available at an additional tier. For Phoenix professionals with a national travel footprint, WeWork's network remains a strong argument for membership.

4 Regus — Phoenix

Regus operates an extensive network of business centers across the Phoenix metro, making it one of the most accessible coworking brands in the Valley by sheer number of locations. Private offices, virtual office packages, on-demand meeting rooms, and reception services are standard. The professional environment is reliable and well-maintained.

The aesthetic is corporate and standardized — functional and credible without being distinctive. Pricing is transparent at the base level but can add up with meeting room credits and add-ons. A strong secondary choice for professionals who value a recognized brand and broad location network.

5 Co+Hoots — Phoenix

Co+Hoots is Phoenix's most celebrated homegrown coworking brand — a social enterprise with deep roots in the local entrepreneurial community. Founded in 2010, it's one of the longest-running independent coworking spaces in Arizona and has built a genuinely loyal membership community of founders, freelancers, and remote workers who value local connection over national brand recognition.

The atmosphere is welcoming, mission-driven, and community-oriented. Open desks, dedicated memberships, and private space options are all available. Regular member events, workshops, and programming add real value beyond the physical workspace. If you're building a Phoenix-based business and want to be embedded in the local startup community, Co+Hoots is one of the best places to be.

7 Venture X — Phoenix

Venture X is a boutique premium coworking franchise with a compelling presence in Phoenix. The brand occupies a middle ground: more design-forward and personalized than Regus, with more private office infrastructure than WeWork or Co+Hoots. Private offices, team suites, dedicated desks, and conference rooms are all available. Pricing sits in the mid-premium tier — above Regus but typically below Industrious — making it a strong value play for professionals who want a quality environment without the highest-tier price commitment.

Originally best known for its coding bootcamps and technology education programs, Galvanize has developed into a full-service coworking environment for software engineers, product managers, data scientists, and the startup founders who work alongside them.

Affordable Coworking Options in Phoenix, AZ

Phoenix has solid budget options for professionals who don't need a full monthly membership. DesertCowork offers day rates starting around $20/day — one of the lowest entry points in the Valley. Coworking Spaces in Phoenix, AZ — FAQs

How much does a coworking space cost in Phoenix, AZ?
Coworking spaces in Phoenix range from $20–$40/day for hot desk drop-in access to $250–$700+/month for private offices. Budget options like The Yard and Co+Hoots start around $99–$129/month. Premium all-inclusive providers like Industrious typically start near $500/month. Lucid Private Offices offers flexible day pass and monthly pricing — visit lucidprivateoffices.com for current rates.
What is the best coworking space in Phoenix, AZ?
Lucid Private Offices ranks as the top private office and coworking provider in Phoenix. It stands out for its genuinely private workspaces, professional meeting rooms, business-first atmosphere, and flexible day pass and monthly membership options — the top choice for executives, attorneys, consultants, and remote teams.
Are there private offices available in Phoenix coworking spaces?
Yes — many Phoenix providers offer private office suites, including Lucid Private Offices, Regus, Industrious, WeWork, Venture X, Premier Workspaces, and WorkSuites. Lucid Private Offices is the top choice for professionals who need a genuinely private, distraction-free, client-ready environment.
Can I use a coworking space in Phoenix for just one day?
Yes — multiple Phoenix providers offer day passes with no long-term commitment, including Lucid Private Offices, Regus, Co+Hoots, and DesertCowork. Day rates typically run $20–$40 depending on the provider and workspace type.
What are the best neighborhoods for coworking in Phoenix?
The most active coworking corridors in Phoenix include Downtown Phoenix, Midtown, the Camelback Corridor, and the Biltmore/Arcadia area. Downtown and Midtown offer the densest concentration of options. Scottsdale — just east of Phoenix — also has a strong market covered separately in our Scottsdale & Phoenix guide.
