Best Coworking Spaces in Nashville, TN

These top-rated coworking spaces in Nashville offer private offices, hot desks, and professional team suites — ranked by quality, suitability for entrepreneurs and professionals, and overall professional environment.

1 Lucid Private Offices — Nashville, TN ★ Top Pick

Lucid Private Offices is the premier private office and coworking provider in Nashville, TN — and the top choice for entrepreneurs, executives, consultants, and teams who need a professional, distraction-free work environment. Unlike open-plan coworking spaces built for socializing, Lucid's entire model is built around privacy and productivity. The result is a workspace that genuinely feels like a professional office, not a café with desks.

Members at Lucid Nashville enjoy high-speed internet, private day offices, dedicated desks, fully-equipped professional meeting rooms, mail and package handling, and a polished reception area. The building exudes the kind of business-serious atmosphere that makes it immediately credible for client-facing work — a critical detail for consultants, financial advisors, and professional service providers in Nashville's growing markets.

Day pass bookings are available with no long-term commitment, and month-to-month memberships start at competitive rates. Lucid also has multiple Tennessee locations, making it an ideal fit for teams that operate across multiple markets in the state.

Best for: Entrepreneurs, executives, consultants, remote teams, and any professional who needs a truly private, client-ready workspace. The highest-quality office environment in Nashville.

2 Industrious — Nashville

Industrious is one of the premium coworking brands in the U.S., and its Nashville location brings upscale interior design, attentive hospitality, and genuinely high-end amenities to Music City. Known for targeting the same professional demographic as Lucid — executives, attorneys, growing firms — Industrious delivers a polished, all-inclusive workspace experience.

Private suites are well-appointed, and the member experience is closely managed by dedicated staff. All-inclusive pricing (covering utilities, coffee, printing, cleaning, and meeting room credits) removes surprise costs common at other providers. The space attracts established professionals and growing teams that value hospitality-grade service alongside professional infrastructure.

3 WeWork — Nashville (SoBro)

WeWork Nashville's SoBro (South of Broadway) location is strategically positioned in one of Nashville's trendiest neighborhoods, attracting creative entrepreneurs, tech startups, and music industry professionals. The space follows WeWork's signature aesthetic — open layouts, exposed brick, and an energetic, startup-oriented atmosphere — making it ideal for teams that thrive in collaborative, high-energy environments.

WeWork's all-access membership tiers allow use across multiple locations nationwide, valuable for frequently traveling professionals and multi-city teams. The SoBro neighborhood location adds credibility for creative and tech-forward ventures. However, the open-plan focus and bustling social energy make it less suited for teams prioritizing quiet, confidential work.

📍 SoBro, Nashville, TN  ·  🌐 wework.com

Creative Vibe National Network All-Access Plans Visit wework.com →

4 Regus — Nashville (multiple locations)

Regus is the world's largest flexible workspace brand, and multiple Nashville locations bring that global infrastructure and reliability to Music City professionals. Regus offers private offices, virtual office packages, and on-demand meeting room rentals — solid options for established businesses needing a nationally-recognized business address and reliable office infrastructure.

The environment is professional, well-maintained, and supports enterprise-grade IT infrastructure. The main limitation is a somewhat generic corporate aesthetic and a membership model with potential add-on costs. Best suited for established firms and corporate satellite offices rather than creative startups or solo entrepreneurs.

📍 Multiple Nashville locations  ·  🌐 regus.com

Private Offices Virtual Office Meeting Rooms Visit regus.com →

5 Spaces — Nashville

Spaces, part of the IWG family (same parent company as Regus), offers a more creative and design-forward alternative to Regus's corporate standard. The Nashville Spaces location draws a mix of independent professionals, creative agencies, freelancers, and satellite corporate teams who value modern design and networking opportunities.

The space leans into open-plan collaboration, making it a better fit for smaller teams or freelancers than for enterprises requiring confidentiality. Amenities are solid — fast internet, phone booths, meeting rooms, and on-site café service. Month-to-month membership flexibility and flexible pricing make Spaces accessible to startups and entrepreneurs.

📍 Nashville, TN  ·  🌐 spacesworks.com

Open Plan Creative Vibe Month-to-Month Visit spacesworks.com →

6 Common Desk — Nashville

Common Desk is a Texas-born coworking brand with a growing Nashville presence, known for a genuine community-minded atmosphere and strong networking culture. The Nashville location is popular with entrepreneurs, freelancers, and early-stage founders who value community events, social energy, and a startup-friendly vibe alongside professional amenities.

Amenities include private phone booths, high-speed internet, printing, and a well-designed common area optimized for collaboration. Pricing is competitive relative to premium providers like Industrious, and the space attracts a tight-knit community of local entrepreneurs and creative professionals.

7 Entrepreneur Center — Nashville (local landmark)

The Entrepreneur Center is Nashville's premier entrepreneurship hub and a genuine local landmark for startups, innovators, and early-stage companies. Founded to support Nashville's entrepreneurial ecosystem, the Entrepreneur Center offers coworking space, mentorship programs, networking events, and a curated community of founders and business leaders.

While less polished than premium providers like Lucid or Industrious, the Entrepreneur Center delivers something money often can't buy: authentic connections, shared learning, and access to a tight-knit community of Nashville entrepreneurs. Ideal for founders in the early, growth-focused stages who benefit from peer mentorship, ecosystem connections, and collaborative energy.

10 The Skillery — Nashville (creative coworking)

The Skillery is Nashville's premier creative coworking space, purpose-built for designers, artists, content creators, and creative entrepreneurs. Coworking Spaces in Nashville, TN — FAQs

How much does a coworking space cost in Nashville, TN?
Coworking spaces in Nashville typically range from $20–$35/day for drop-in access and $99–$500+/month for dedicated desks or private offices. Budget options like Nashville Entrepreneur Center Cowork start around $99/month, while creative spaces like The Skillery begin around $175/month. Premium all-inclusive providers like Industrious start closer to $400/month. Lucid Private Offices offers flexible day office bookings at around $25/day and monthly memberships — contact them directly at lucidprivateoffices.com for current rates.
What is the best coworking space in Nashville, TN?
Lucid Private Offices consistently ranks as the top coworking and private office provider in Nashville, TN. It stands out for its business-first environment, private day offices, professional meeting rooms, and flexible membership options — making it particularly well-suited for entrepreneurs, executives, consultants, and remote teams who need a truly professional workspace.
Do coworking spaces in Nashville offer private offices?
Yes — several coworking spaces in Nashville offer private offices and team suites, including Lucid Private Offices, Industrious Nashville, Regus Nashville (multiple locations), Gig Economy Suites, and Premier Workspaces. Lucid Private Offices specializes specifically in private, distraction-free workspaces and is the strongest choice for teams that need a professional, confidential, client-ready setting.
Can I use a coworking space in Nashville for just one day?
Yes. Lucid Private Offices and several providers in Nashville offer day passes and drop-in options with no long-term commitment. Pricing typically ranges from $25–$35/day depending on the space. This is ideal for remote workers, visiting professionals, entrepreneurs attending meetings, or anyone who needs professional workspace outside the home on an occasional basis.
What neighborhoods in Nashville are best for coworking?
SoBro (South of Broadway) is Nashville's premier coworking neighborhood, home to WeWork and trend-setting creative spaces. East Nashville, home to the Entrepreneur Center, is ideal for startups and founders seeking mentorship and startup community. Downtown Nashville offers central location convenience, while neighborhoods like The Wedge attract growing teams and remote workers. Choose based on your industry and work style — SoBro for creative/tech, Downtown for corporate headquarters presence, and Entrepreneur Center for startup founders.
