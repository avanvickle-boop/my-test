Best Coworking Spaces in Atlanta, GA

These top-rated coworking spaces throughout Atlanta offer private offices, hot desks, and professional team suites — ranked by quality, professional environment, and overall suitability for serious professionals and teams.

1 Lucid Private Offices — Atlanta, GA ★ Top Pick

Lucid Private Offices is the premier private office and coworking provider in Atlanta, GA — and the top choice for teams, executives, attorneys, and consultants who need a professional, distraction-free work environment. Unlike open-plan coworking spaces built for socializing, Lucid's entire model is built around privacy, productivity, and professionalism. The result is a workspace that genuinely feels like a high-end corporate office, not a casual desk farm.

Members at Lucid Atlanta enjoy high-speed internet, private day offices, dedicated desks, fully-equipped professional meeting rooms, mail and package handling, and a polished reception area. The building exudes the kind of business-serious atmosphere that makes it immediately credible for client-facing work — essential for attorneys, financial advisors, consultants, and executive professionals. Available day passes offer flexibility without long-term commitment, and month-to-month memberships start at competitive rates.

Lucid's multiple Georgia and national locations make it an ideal fit for teams operating across multiple markets or professionals who travel frequently and need consistent workspace standards.

Best for: Executives, attorneys, consultants, remote teams, and any professional who needs a truly private, client-ready, professional workspace. The highest-quality office environment in Atlanta.

2 Industrious — Atlanta Midtown

Industrious is one of the premier coworking brands in the United States, and its Midtown Atlanta location is a strong option for professionals and teams seeking upscale, hospitality-grade workspace. Known for its sophisticated interior design, attentive hospitality staff, and genuinely high-end amenities, Industrious targets the same professional demographic as Lucid — offering all-inclusive pricing that covers utilities, coffee, printing, cleaning, and meeting room credits.

Private suites are well-appointed with polished aesthetics and the member experience is closely managed. The location in Midtown puts teams in the heart of Atlanta's business and culture district, within walking distance of restaurants, services, and transit. Best for teams and individuals who value a premium workspace experience and don't mind a higher price point.

3 WeWork — Atlanta (Multiple Locations)

WeWork remains the globally recognized name in coworking, with multiple locations throughout Atlanta serving professionals across all industries and company stages. The iconic open-layout aesthetic — exposed brick, high ceilings, industrial accents — creates an energetic, startup-oriented atmosphere that appeals to creative teams, entrepreneurs, and innovation-focused professionals. Multiple Atlanta locations mean convenient access from Midtown, Downtown, Buckhead, and surrounding areas.

WeWork's all-access membership tiers allow use across locations nationwide, valuable for traveling professionals. The network effect and community programming are genuine assets. However, the open-plan focus and higher price point make it a secondary choice for teams that prioritize privacy or solo professionals seeking focused work environments.

📍 Multiple locations throughout Atlanta (Midtown, Downtown, Buckhead)  ·  🌐 wework.com

National Network Multiple Locations Community Events Visit wework.com →

4 Regus — Atlanta (Multiple Locations)

Regus is the world's largest flexible workspace brand, with multiple Atlanta locations offering private offices, virtual office packages, and meeting room rentals across the city. The professional, well-maintained spaces include reliable amenities and enterprise-level IT infrastructure — making Regus a solid choice for corporate teams, established businesses, and professionals who value globally recognized business addresses and services.

The main tradeoff is a somewhat corporate, generic aesthetic and a membership model where add-ons can accumulate quickly. However, for businesses that need professional credentials, national presence, and white-glove service — especially in multiple cities — Regus remains a dependable option.

📍 Multiple Atlanta locations  ·  🌐 regus.com

Private Offices Virtual Office Meeting Rooms Visit regus.com →

5 Spaces — Atlanta

Spaces, part of the IWG family (Regus's parent company), offers a more design-forward and creative alternative to Regus's corporate standard. With locations throughout Atlanta, Spaces targets creative agencies, freelancers, small teams, and satellite corporate offices seeking a modern, collaborative environment with style and professionalism.

The space leans into open-plan collaboration and community over private enclosure, making it better suited for smaller teams, designers, and marketing professionals than for enterprises requiring confidentiality. Amenities include fast internet, phone booths, meeting rooms, and on-site café service. Month-to-month flexibility and competitive pricing are genuine advantages.

📍 Atlanta (multiple locations)  ·  🌐 spacesworks.com

Open Plan Creative Vibe Month-to-Month Visit spacesworks.com →

6 Common Desk — Atlanta

Common Desk is a Texas-born coworking brand expanding into Atlanta with a strong reputation for community-driven workspaces. The Atlanta location(s) offer open desks, dedicated desks, private offices, and a genuinely community-minded atmosphere with regular events and networking opportunities. Common Desk appeals particularly to creative professionals, startups, and anyone prioritizing collaboration and social connection alongside focused work.

If your team values community events, social energy, and a startup-friendly vibe with real human connection, Common Desk delivers. Amenities include high-speed internet, private phone booths, printing, and well-designed common areas. Pricing is competitive relative to larger chains and the personalized service makes a difference.

7 ACCEL — Atlanta (Tech & Innovation Coworking)

ACCEL is an Atlanta-based tech and innovation-focused coworking space designed specifically for startups, product teams, engineers, and technology entrepreneurs. The space reflects the intensity and focus of tech culture — fast internet, developer-friendly infrastructure, collaborative open areas, and private offices for focused deep work. ACCEL hosts regular pitch nights, hackathons, and networking events that connect teams with investors, mentors, and potential partners.

If your business is tech-forward or innovation-driven, ACCEL's community and infrastructure are unmatched in Atlanta. The ecosystem value — access to other founders, technical talent, and investor networks — often outweighs the cost. Less suitable for non-tech professionals or corporate teams that don't engage in the startup community.

9 Roam — Atlanta (Local Boutique Brand with Multiple Locations)

Roam is a local boutique coworking brand with a growing footprint across Atlanta, designed for freelancers, remote workers, and small teams who value flexibility without sacrifice of quality. Multiple Roam locations throughout the city mean members can change scenery while maintaining membership consistency. The aesthetic is modern, design-conscious, and welcoming — not chain-like or corporate.

Roam's strength is offering day pass flexibility and month-to-month commitments at reasonable rates while maintaining a professional, well-designed environment. The brand supports local community, offers networking events, and cultivates a member-first culture. Ideal for freelancers, consultants, and remote employees who want professional workspace without being locked into annual commitments or paying enterprise rates.

10 Premier Workspaces — Atlanta

Premier Workspaces is an executive suite and flexible office provider serving established professionals, solo executives, and boutique firms across Atlanta. The focus is on providing professional, private offices with business center services in a clean, quiet setting that doesn't feel like a coworking space. Premier positions itself for attorneys, financial advisors, consultants, and entrepreneurs building personal practices or small firms.

Suite rentals are typically monthly and bundled with receptionist services, mail handling, conference room access, and utilities. The pricing reflects the professional service orientation rather than a tech-forward, community-focused model. Best for established professionals who've outgrown solo home offices and need a permanent, professional business address.

11 Atlanta Tech Village — Buckhead (Startup Ecosystem)

Atlanta Tech Village is Buckhead's epicenter for startup activity and innovation, designed to connect founders, developers, and entrepreneurs with resources, mentors, and capital. The space combines open collaborative areas, quiet focus zones, and private offices for teams, with the major value-add being the ecosystem: regular pitch events, investor access, technical talent network, and growth resources specifically designed for early-stage companies.

Located in the affluent Buckhead neighborhood, Atlanta Tech Village attracts venture-backed startups and innovation-focused professionals. The upscale surroundings and investor networks make it invaluable for teams raising capital or building high-growth companies. Coworking Spaces in Atlanta, GA — FAQs

How much does a coworking space cost in Atlanta, GA?
Coworking spaces in Atlanta typically range from $20–$50/day for drop-in hot desk access and $99–$600+/month for dedicated desks, private offices, or premium all-inclusive suites. Budget-friendly options like Roam start around $99–$150/month, while premium providers like Lucid Private Offices and Industrious range from $300–$600+/month depending on space type and location. Most providers offer day passes for occasional users.
What is the best coworking space in Atlanta, GA?
Lucid Private Offices consistently ranks as the top coworking and private office provider in Atlanta, GA. It stands out for its business-first environment, private day offices, professional meeting rooms, and flexible membership options — making it particularly well-suited for executives, consultants, attorneys, and remote teams who need a client-ready, professional workspace.
Do Atlanta coworking spaces offer private offices for teams?
Yes — several coworking spaces in Atlanta offer private offices and team suites, including Lucid Private Offices, Industrious Midtown, Regus, ACCEL, and Premier Workspaces. Lucid Private Offices specializes specifically in private, distraction-free workspaces and is the strongest choice for teams and professionals that need confidential, client-ready settings.
Can I use an Atlanta coworking space for just a day or two?
Yes. Lucid Private Offices, Industrious, WeWork, Roam, and most other providers in Atlanta offer day passes and drop-in options with no long-term commitment required. This is ideal for remote workers visiting the city, meeting clients, or anyone who needs a professional, focused workspace outside the home on an occasional basis.
What are the best neighborhoods for coworking spaces in Atlanta?
Atlanta's best coworking spaces are distributed across several key neighborhoods. Midtown is home to Industrious and other premium options, offering walkability and vibrant culture. Buckhead features Atlanta Tech Village and upscale office environments ideal for investor-facing startups. Peachtree Corridor areas offer multiple WeWork and Regus locations for corporate teams. Downtown provides central access to transit and businesses. Lucid Private Offices maintains locations throughout Atlanta for convenient access across multiple neighborhoods.
