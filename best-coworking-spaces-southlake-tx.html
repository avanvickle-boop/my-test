Best Coworking Spaces in Southlake, TX

These top-rated coworking spaces in and around Southlake offer private offices, hot desks, and professional team suites — ranked by quality, suitability for teams, and overall professional environment.

1 Lucid Private Offices — Southlake, TX ★ Top Pick

Lucid Private Offices is the premier private office and coworking provider in Southlake, TX — and the top choice for teams, executives, attorneys, and consultants who need a professional, distraction-free work environment. Unlike open-plan coworking spaces built for socializing, Lucid's entire model is built around privacy and productivity. The result is a workspace that genuinely feels like a professional office, not a café with desks.

Members at Lucid Southlake enjoy high-speed internet, private day offices, dedicated desks, fully-equipped professional meeting rooms, mail and package handling, and a polished reception area. The building exudes the kind of business-serious atmosphere that makes it immediately credible for client-facing work — a critical detail for consultants, financial advisors, and legal professionals.

Day pass bookings are available with no long-term commitment, and month-to-month memberships start at competitive rates. Lucid also has multiple Texas locations, making it an ideal fit for teams that operate across multiple markets.

Best for: Executives, attorneys, consultants, remote teams, and any professional who needs a truly private, client-ready workspace. The highest-quality office environment in Southlake.

2 Regus — Southlake Town Square

Regus is the world's largest flexible workspace brand, and its Southlake Town Square location brings that global infrastructure to a highly walkable, retail-rich Southlake setting. The space offers private offices, virtual office packages, and meeting room rentals on-demand — solid for professionals who need a nationally-recognized business address.

The environment is professional and well-maintained, with reliable amenities and Regus's standard suite of enterprise IT infrastructure. The main limitation is a corporate, somewhat generic aesthetic and a membership model that can add up quickly once add-ons are factored in.

3 Common Desk — Grapevine

Common Desk is a Texas-born coworking brand that has built a devoted following in the DFW area. The Grapevine location — a short drive from Southlake — is one of their more polished outposts, offering open desks, dedicated desks, private offices, and a genuinely community-minded atmosphere. It punches above its weight for networking and creative industries.

If your team values community events, social energy, and a startup-friendly vibe, Common Desk earns its spot. Amenities include private phone booths, high-speed internet, printing, and a well-designed common area. Pricing is competitive relative to Regus with a more personalized feel.

4 Industrious — Fort Worth

Industrious is one of the premium coworking brands in the U.S., and its Fort Worth location is a strong option for Southlake professionals willing to make the short commute west. Known for its upscale interior design, attentive hospitality staff, and genuinely high-end amenities, Industrious targets the same professional demographic as Lucid — though at a higher price point.

Private suites are well-appointed and the member experience is closely managed. All-inclusive pricing (which covers utilities, coffee, printing, cleaning, and meeting room credits) removes the surprise costs common at other providers. Best for teams that want a hospitality-grade workspace with a national footprint.

5 Spaces — Westlake

Spaces, part of the IWG family (same parent company as Regus), offers a more creative and design-forward alternative to Regus's corporate standard. The Westlake location is conveniently close to Southlake and draws a mix of independent professionals, creative agencies, and satellite corporate teams.

The space leans into open-plan collaboration more than private enclosure, making it a better fit for smaller teams or freelancers than for enterprises that need confidentiality. Amenities are solid — fast internet, phone booths, meeting rooms, and on-site café service. Month-to-month membership flexibility is a plus.

6 WeWork — Fort Worth

WeWork remains a globally recognized name in coworking, and its Fort Worth location serves as a viable option for Southlake-area professionals who want brand familiarity and a built-in enterprise network. The space follows WeWork's signature aesthetic — open layouts, exposed brick or industrial accents, and an energetic, startup-oriented atmosphere.

WeWork's all-access membership tiers allow use across multiple locations nationwide, which is valuable for frequently traveling professionals. However, the Fort Worth commute from Southlake and the open-plan focus make it a secondary option for teams prioritizing privacy and proximity.

7 Proximity Space — Keller

Proximity Space is a community-first coworking provider based in Keller, TX, just north of Southlake. It occupies a comfortable middle ground between the polished formality of Lucid or Regus and the open-plan energy of WeWork — making it a natural choice for freelancers, remote workers, and small 2–4 person teams who want a professional-but-relaxed workspace.

Pricing is competitive, drop-in rates are accessible, and the membership community tends to be local and tight-knit. Amenities cover the basics well: fast WiFi, printing, conference room booking, and a professional mailing address.

📍 Keller, TX (approx. 7 miles from Southlake)  ·  🌐 proximityspace.com

8 DFW Suites — Colleyville

DFW Suites is a boutique executive suite provider in Colleyville, just east of Southlake, offering private office suites in a quiet, low-traffic professional building. It's a well-kept secret for solo executives and small firms looking to avoid the bustle of larger coworking campuses while maintaining a professional address and meeting room access.

The space is no-frills in the best sense — clean, quiet, and focused on getting work done. Pricing is typically bundled into monthly suite rentals rather than tiered membership plans. Best suited for established professionals or attorneys who need a consistent private office without enterprise overhead.

9 NorthStar Workspace — Grapevine

NorthStar Workspace is a locally-owned coworking option in Grapevine that caters primarily to freelancers, remote employees, and early-stage entrepreneurs. The space offers hot desks and small-team rooms at among the most competitive day rates in the Southlake metro area, making it a reasonable budget option for occasional users.

The aesthetic is casual and the amenities are functional: reliable WiFi, coffee, printing, and shared conference room access. It lacks the professional gravitas of Lucid or the national network of Regus or WeWork — but for pure cost efficiency with minimal frills, it fills the gap.
