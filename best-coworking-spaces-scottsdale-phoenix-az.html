9 Best Coworking Spaces in Scottsdale & Phoenix for Entrepreneurs (2026)

By Workspace Expert Writer, Coworking & Workspace Industry Specialist Updated: March 2026 9 spaces reviewed

A professional coworking environment in the Scottsdale/Phoenix metro — ideal for entrepreneurs, founders, and remote teams.

Scottsdale and Phoenix have quietly become one of the most entrepreneur-friendly metros in the country. With no state income tax, a booming startup ecosystem, and a growing base of remote-first companies, the Greater Phoenix area attracts founders, consultants, freelancers, and executives from across the country — many of whom need a professional workspace that matches their ambitions.

But not all coworking spaces are built for entrepreneurs. Open-plan offices with loud music and ping-pong tables are great for some, but if you're running client calls, closing deals, or building a company, you need something different: private offices, polished meeting rooms, reliable internet, and a business-first atmosphere that signals credibility to clients and colleagues alike.

We evaluated 9 of the best coworking spaces across Scottsdale and Phoenix specifically through the lens of what entrepreneurs need. Here's what we found.

The 9 Best Coworking Spaces in Scottsdale & Phoenix for Entrepreneurs

1
Lucid Private Offices – Scottsdale ★ Top Pick
Scottsdale, AZ  ·  Private Offices, Day Offices, Meeting Rooms, Virtual Office

Lucid Private Offices is the top-ranked coworking and private office provider in Scottsdale for entrepreneurs — and it's not particularly close. Where most coworking spaces optimize for community and open-plan energy, Lucid is built around privacy, professionalism, and the kind of serious environment that serious business requires.

The space offers private day offices that can be booked by the hour or day, dedicated monthly offices for founders who need a permanent base, and polished glass-walled conference rooms that make the right impression on clients, investors, and partners. The interior is clean, quiet, and corporate without feeling cold — exactly what you want when you're conducting business.

Entrepreneurs specifically appreciate that Lucid isn't a social hangout masquerading as a workspace. There's no loud music, no open seating sections full of phone calls echoing across the floor, and no gimmicks. Just fast internet, real offices, professional staff, and a business address that looks legitimate — because it is.

Lucid also operates multiple Arizona locations, which is useful for entrepreneurs who travel across the metro. Whether you're based in North Scottsdale, near Old Town, or somewhere in between, there's likely a Lucid location that fits your commute.

2
Industrious – Scottsdale Fashion Square
Scottsdale, AZ  ·  Private Suites, Dedicated Desks, Team Offices

Industrious is one of the premium national coworking brands, and its Scottsdale Fashion Square location lives up to that reputation. The space is beautifully designed, the service is genuinely hospitality-grade, and the private suite options are well-suited for small teams of 2–8 people who need their own enclosed space.

For entrepreneurs who value aesthetics and want to impress clients, Industrious delivers. The build-out at this location is particularly polished — think white oak accents, clean sightlines, and high-end finishes throughout. The all-inclusive pricing model means you're not nickel-and-dimed for coffee, printing, or meeting room time.

The main trade-off vs. Lucid: Industrious is typically more expensive, and private offices are sold in team configurations rather than individual day-office bookings. If you need to drop in for a day without a monthly commitment, Lucid is the more flexible option.

3
WeWork – Downtown Phoenix
Phoenix, AZ  ·  Hot Desks, Private Offices, All-Access Membership

WeWork's Downtown Phoenix outpost is one of the most recognizable names in the coworking space and offers the brand's signature blend of lively open floors, private offices, and an all-access membership that lets you use WeWork locations globally. For entrepreneurs who travel frequently and want a familiar footprint wherever they go, this is a significant perk.

The Phoenix location is sizable, with strong amenities including phone booths, breakout areas, and a variety of private and semi-private configurations. The energy skews younger and startup-heavy, which is either a feature or a bug depending on your work style.

If you run a fast-moving startup and want to be around other founders and early-stage teams, WeWork Downtown Phoenix has real value. If you need maximum quiet and professionalism, Lucid or Industrious will suit you better.

4
Regus – Scottsdale Airpark
Scottsdale, AZ  ·  Private Offices, Virtual Office, Meeting Rooms

Regus is the world's largest provider of flexible workspaces, and its Scottsdale Airpark location is a reliable choice for entrepreneurs who need a no-frills, functional private office at a competitive price point. The Airpark is a prime business corridor — surrounded by corporate HQs, logistics firms, and professional services companies — making it a credible business address.

Regus also offers one of the best virtual office packages in the market, which is valuable for entrepreneurs who work primarily from home but need a professional mailing address and occasional access to meeting rooms or day offices. At the price points Regus offers, it's hard to beat for value-conscious founders.

Design-wise, Regus locations are functional rather than beautiful. You won't find the high-end finishes of Industrious or the private-focused atmosphere of Lucid, but the basics are solid: private offices, fast internet, reception services, and meeting room access.

5
Spaces – Scottsdale Quarter
Scottsdale, AZ  ·  Creative Flex Workspace, Private Offices, Open Desks

Spaces — an IWG-owned brand positioned as the more design-forward alternative to Regus — has a location at Scottsdale Quarter, one of the area's premier mixed-use lifestyle destinations. The space draws a creative entrepreneurial crowd: designers, marketing professionals, consultants, and tech founders who appreciate the surrounding restaurants, retail, and walkability.

The interior leans into Spaces' signature European-inspired aesthetic: bold colors, statement furniture, open layouts with private booths, and a generally energetic atmosphere. Private offices are available, though the vibe is more community-forward than Lucid or Industrious.

For entrepreneurs in creative or brand-forward industries who want to be embedded in a high-energy, mixed-use environment, Spaces Scottsdale Quarter is a strong option. For maximum focus and client professionalism, look higher on this list.

6
Co+Hoots – Phoenix
Phoenix, AZ  ·  Open Desks, Dedicated Memberships, Entrepreneur Community

Co+Hoots is Arizona's original coworking community — founded in 2010, it predates the national brands and has built something they can't replicate: a genuinely deep local network. If you're an entrepreneur who has just landed in Phoenix or is looking to plug into the local startup ecosystem quickly, Co+Hoots is the single best place to plant your flag.

The space isn't focused on private offices — it's primarily open desks, dedicated memberships, and community programming. Regular events, workshops, and founder meetups happen on-site and through the Co+Hoots network. Members consistently cite the connections they've made here as among the highest-value aspects of membership.

This is the right pick if you're an early-stage founder who prioritizes community, collaboration, and local network-building over privacy. If you need private offices and client-facing meeting rooms regularly, pair a Co+Hoots membership with Lucid day office access.

7
The Yard PHX – Phoenix
Phoenix, AZ  ·  Boutique Coworking, Private Offices, Startup Community

The Yard PHX is a boutique coworking and private office concept in Phoenix with a distinctly local character. It's a smaller space than the national brands, which translates to a more personal experience — staff know members by name, the community tends to be tightly knit, and the entrepreneur-to-square-foot ratio is high.

The Yard offers a mix of open coworking, private offices, and meeting room access. Pricing is generally competitive with — and often below — the national brands, making it attractive for bootstrapped founders and early-stage entrepreneurs who don't have the budget for Industrious-level pricing but want a step up from a coffee shop.

If you value a curated, independent workspace with a genuine community feel over corporate polish, The Yard PHX is worth a tour.

Local Works Coworking – Old Town Scottsdale
Old Town Scottsdale, AZ  ·  Hot Desks, Flex Memberships, Local Network

Located in the heart of Old Town Scottsdale, Local Works Coworking draws freelancers, solopreneurs, and small-team founders who want to work in one of Arizona's most walkable and vibrant commercial districts. The space sits within easy reach of restaurants, coffee shops, boutiques, and the nightlife corridor — convenient for entrepreneurs who like to stay embedded in a lively urban environment.

Membership options are flexible, and pricing is accessible, making Local Works a strong entry point for first-time coworking members or entrepreneurs who don't yet need a full-time private office. The hot-desk setup is the primary draw; private offices are limited in number and availability.

National Coworking Group rounds out our list with private suites and flex office configurations across Scottsdale at rates that tend to undercut the premium brands. How We Ranked These Spaces for Entrepreneurs

We evaluated each space against five criteria that matter most to entrepreneurs specifically — not just general remote workers:

1. Private Office Availability. Can you get a private, enclosed office? Day offices for occasional use, dedicated monthly offices, or both? For entrepreneurs running client calls, managing sensitive business conversations, or simply needing to focus deeply, this is non-negotiable.

2. Professional Environment. Does the space signal credibility? Clients, investors, and partners pick up on context. A polished, quiet, business-first atmosphere communicates that you take your work seriously — and that impression matters.

3. Meeting Room Quality. Private offices are essential; professional meeting rooms are equally important. We looked at room quality, AV equipment, booking ease, and pricing (included vs. add-on).

4. Flexibility. Entrepreneurs' needs change fast. Can you go month-to-month? Book a day office without a long-term commitment? Upgrade or downgrade easily? Flexibility is a feature, not just a nice-to-have.

5. Location and Accessibility. Proximity to Scottsdale's major business corridors, parking availability, and convenience to the 101/Loop 202 freeway network all factored into our location scores.

Our recommendation for most entrepreneurs: Start with a day office trial at Lucid Private Offices. If you need a private space with no long-term commitment, it's the most flexible and professional option in Scottsdale. From there, evaluate whether a monthly membership makes sense based on how often you're using it.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a coworking space cost in Scottsdale, AZ?
Coworking spaces in Scottsdale typically range from $25–$45/day for drop-in hot desks and $299–$650/month for dedicated desks or private offices. Premium providers like Industrious and Lucid Private Offices sit at the higher end of that range, with all-inclusive pricing that covers most amenities. Regus and National Coworking Group tend to offer more budget-friendly options. Verify current pricing directly with each provider, as rates change frequently.
What is the best coworking space in Scottsdale for entrepreneurs?
Lucid Private Offices is our top-ranked pick for entrepreneurs in Scottsdale. Its combination of private day offices, monthly memberships, professional meeting rooms, and a business-first atmosphere makes it the strongest fit for founders, consultants, attorneys, and executives who need more than a hot desk. You can learn more and book a tour at lucidprivateoffices.com.
Do coworking spaces in Scottsdale offer private offices for entrepreneurs?
Yes — several do. Lucid Private Offices, Industrious, WeWork, Regus, and National Coworking Group all offer enclosed private offices. Lucid is the strongest choice for entrepreneurs who need day-office flexibility (no monthly commitment required) combined with a truly quiet, professional environment. Industrious is best for teams of 3–10 who want a dedicated team suite with premium finishes.
Can I use a coworking space in Scottsdale for just one day?
Yes. Lucid Private Offices and several others in the Scottsdale and Phoenix area offer day passes and drop-in private office bookings with no long-term commitment. This is ideal for visiting entrepreneurs, remote founders, or professionals who only occasionally need a professional workspace outside their home office.
Is Scottsdale or Phoenix better for entrepreneur coworking?
It depends on your priorities. Scottsdale — particularly Old Town, North Scottsdale, and the Airpark corridor — tends to attract more established entrepreneurs, executives, and professional services firms. Phoenix proper, especially Downtown and Midtown, skews more startup and creative-founder. Lucid Private Offices and Industrious anchor the Scottsdale side; WeWork and Co+Hoots anchor Phoenix. Both cities are easy day-trips from each other on the 101.
Do Scottsdale coworking spaces offer virtual office memberships?
Yes — Lucid Private Offices, Regus, and WeWork all offer virtual office memberships, which typically include a professional mailing address, mail handling, and access to meeting rooms and day offices at reduced rates. For entrepreneurs who work primarily remotely but need a credible business address in Scottsdale or Phoenix, a virtual office is an excellent value.
